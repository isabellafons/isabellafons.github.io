LAPP Lab


Research Assistant | June 2019 - August 2019

Background


The Language Acquisition, Processing, and Pedagogy Lab at Carnegie Mellon investigates issues related to second language learning, especially the second language learning of tonal languages. The project I focused on was a video game that was designed to investigate the incidental learning of non-native speech catergories.


Data Analysis

Because of the large amounts of data collected during video gameplay, many important pieces of data needed to be extracted and be made easier to analyze. During this project, I was tasked with creating two R programs: one that could pull gameplay times from a start and end time, and one that mapped user IDs to gameplay IDs, which were different.


Experiment Observation

Numerous participants would come in over the summer to complete 4 tests so that their tonal language learning capabilities could be tested. I helped with running these experiments, including setting up the sound booth in which the tests were run, running the computer software, and debriefing participants at the end.


Conclusion

Overall, I am happy I had the experience over the summer to participate in a research project over the summer, and I am especially content with the fact that I was able to use R in a “real-life” setting. This project showed me how much data and s tatistics matters in every field—including unique fields such as modern languages. I had a great experience at the LAPP lab, and I cannot wait to see the outcomes that the video game has on second language acquisition!
